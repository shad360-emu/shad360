# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to making participation in the shad360 community a welcoming and respectful experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity or expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Being respectful and considerate of others.
- Accepting constructive feedback gracefully.
- Focusing discussions on technical topics and project goals.
- Helping new contributors.
- Respecting different viewpoints and experiences.
- Taking responsibility for mistakes and learning from them.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, discrimination, or personal attacks.
- Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments.
- Threats or intimidation.
- Posting private information without permission.
- Spam or intentionally disruptive behavior.
- Any other conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional community.

---

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for enforcing this Code of Conduct.

They may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, pull requests, issues, discussions, or other contributions that violate these guidelines.

Maintainers may also temporarily or permanently restrict participation from individuals whose behavior is harmful to the community.

---

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it privately to the project maintainers.

Do not use public GitHub issues to report Code of Conduct violations.

Include any relevant information that may help us investigate the situation.

All reports will be reviewed promptly and handled as confidentially as possible.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including:

- GitHub Issues
- Pull Requests
- Discussions
- Discord (if applicable)
- Community chat rooms
- Any other official shad360 community spaces

It also applies when someone is representing the project in public.
